Best Movies that I Remember so Far

Top 3 Movies

I Am Legend

"I Am Legend" is a 2007 post-apocalyptic action thriller directed by Francis Lawrence and starring Will Smith.
The film is loosely based on Richard Matheson's 1954 novel of the same name. Set in New York City, it follows US Army virologist Robert Neville, who is the last human survivor after a virus, initially created to cure cancer, devastates humanity and turns survivors into nocturnal mutants. Immune to the virus, Neville works to develop a cure while battling the mutants. The film was a box office success, praised for Smith's performance, though it drew some criticism for its deviations f rom the novel, particularly its ending. A sequel was announced in 2022, with Smith and Michael B. Jordan set to star.

Train to Busan

"Train to Busan" is a 2016 South Korean action horror film directed by Yeon Sang-ho.
The story follows Seok-woo (Gong Yoo), a workaholic father who boards a high-speed train from Seoul to Busan with his estranged young daughter, Su-an (Kim Su-an), to visit her mother. During the journey, a zombie apocalypse suddenly breaks out, and the passengers must fight for their survival as the train hurtles toward its destination. The film is noted for its intense action sequences, emotional depth, and social commentary, and it received widespread acclaim both domestically and internationally, becoming a significant hit in the zombie genre.

Inside Out

"Inside Out" is a 2015 animated film produced by Pixar Animation Studios and released by Walt Disney Pictures.
Directed by Pete Docter, the movie delves into the emotional world of an 11-year-old girl named Riley, who is uprooted from her Midwest life when her family moves to San Francisco. The story is primarily set inside Riley's mind, where five personified emotions—Joy, Sadness, Anger, Fear, and Disgust—work together to navigate her experiences and reactions to the changes in her life. The film explores themes of emotional growth and the importance of all emotions. "Inside Out" received critical acclaim for its imaginative storytelling, emotional depth, and innovative concept, and it won the Academy Award for Best Animated Feature.
